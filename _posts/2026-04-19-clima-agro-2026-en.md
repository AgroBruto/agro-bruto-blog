# Climate Events in 2026: The Transition to El Niño and Impacts on the Agri-Food Supply Chain

The year 2026 has proven to be a period of intense climate variability, demanding unprecedented adaptability from Brazilian agribusiness. The global climate dynamic is undergoing a significant shift: the weakening of the **La Niña** phenomenon at the beginning of the year, followed by a neutral phase, and the increasing probability of **El Niño**'s return in the second half. According to the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), the chances of El Niño forming between June and August 2026 reach 62% [1].

This climate transition not only affects crop development in the fields but also reverberates throughout the complex agri-food supply chain, from credit concession to export logistics.

## The Climate Scenario and Agricultural Production

Irregular rainfall marked the first quarter of 2026, creating vulnerabilities for essential crops such as soybeans, corn, and coffee [2]. The projected scenario for the rest of the year, under the influence of a possible El Niño, divides Brazil into climatic extremes:

### Projected Regional Impacts:

| Region | Climate Forecast (El Niño) | Production Impacts |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **South** | Above-average rainfall. | Favors the initial development of some crops, but excessive humidity can hinder harvesting, reduce grain quality, and increase the incidence of fungal diseases. |
| **North and Northeast (MATOPIBA)** | High risk of drought and above-average temperatures. | Severe water stress, with potential crop failure similar to that observed in the 2023 crisis. Drastic reduction in grain and cotton productivity [3]. |
| **Midwest and Southeast** | Irregular rainfall and heatwaves. | Delays in corn second-crop planting due to late soybean harvest rains, increasing the risk of losses from frosts or drought at the end of the cycle [4]. |

## Repercussions on the Supply Chain and Logistics

Climate impacts extend far beyond the farm gate. The Brazilian agribusiness supply chain, highly dependent on road and river infrastructure, faces severe logistical challenges in years of climatic anomalies.

**Logistical Bottlenecks in the Northern Arc:** The severe drought projected for the North region, characteristic of El Niño, can drastically reduce river levels in the Amazon Basin. This compromises the navigability of waterways (such as the Madeira and Tapajós Rivers), which are vital routes for transporting crops from the Midwest through the Northern Arc ports. The inability to use barges forces cargo redirection to the Southern and Southeastern ports (Santos and Paranaguá) via roads, increasing freight costs and causing traffic jams.

**Challenges in the South:** Conversely, excessive rainfall in the South region can damage rural roads, hindering the transport of crops from farms to silos and ports, in addition to delaying loading operations at port terminals due to humidity.

**Credit Risk:** Climate volatility also affects the financial market. Financial institutions are re-evaluating rural credit risk, as extreme weather events increase the likelihood of default due to crop failure [5].

## Mitigation Strategies

To navigate this uncertain scenario in 2026, producers and companies in the sector are adopting risk mitigation strategies:

1.  **Precision Agrometeorology:** Investment in hyper-localized climate monitoring technologies to optimize planting windows and input application.
2.  **Rural Insurance:** Increased contracting of agricultural insurance policies to protect invested capital against severe climatic losses.
3.  **Logistical Diversification:** Anticipated planning of alternative outflow routes and greater storage capacity on farms (silos) to await better freight conditions.

## Conclusion

The 2026 climate transition, with the shadow of El Niño on the horizon, reinforces the need for an increasingly resilient and data-driven agribusiness. The ability to anticipate scenarios and adapt both agronomic management and the supply chain logistics will be the differentiator between loss and profitability in this challenging year.
