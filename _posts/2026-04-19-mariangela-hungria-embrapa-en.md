# Mariangela Hungria: The Brazilian 'Nobel' Who Revolutionized Agriculture

Brazilian agribusiness, globally recognized for its productivity and innovation, celebrates one of its greatest minds: **Mariangela Hungria**, a researcher at Embrapa Soja. In 2025, Mariangela was awarded the prestigious **World Food Prize** [1], known as the "Nobel of Agriculture," becoming the first Brazilian woman to receive such an honor. In April 2026, her global recognition was further expanded with her inclusion in TIME magazine's exclusive **TIME 100** list, which highlights the 100 most influential people in the world [2].

## The Revolution of Biological Nitrogen Fixation (BNF)

Mariangela Hungria's work focuses on **Biological Nitrogen Fixation (BNF)**, a natural process where microorganisms, such as *Bradyrhizobium* bacteria (for soybeans) and *Azospirillum* (for other crops), convert atmospheric nitrogen into ammonia, a form that plants can utilize. This technology, developed and refined by Embrapa under Hungria's leadership, allows plants to absorb nitrogen directly from the air, drastically reducing the need for synthetic nitrogen fertilizers [3].

### Economic and Environmental Impact

The benefits of BNF are immeasurable. Economically, Mariangela Hungria's technology generates an estimated **US$ 25 billion per year** in savings for Brazil by avoiding the import of nitrogen fertilizers, which are expensive and whose production is concentrated in a few countries [2].

From an environmental perspective, BNF is a cornerstone of sustainability in agribusiness. The reduction in the use of chemical fertilizers significantly decreases greenhouse gas emissions (mainly nitrous oxide, a potent GHG) and the pollution of water bodies and groundwater, contributing to a cleaner and more efficient production system [3].

## Embrapa: Engine of Tropical Science and Food Sovereignty

Mariangela Hungria's success reflects the excellence of **Embrapa (Brazilian Agricultural Research Corporation)**. Founded in 1973, Embrapa has played a crucial role in transforming Brazil from a food importer into one of the world's largest agricultural exporters. Its research has been fundamental for:

*   **Tropicalization of Agriculture**: Adapting temperate climate crops, such as soybeans and wheat, to Brazilian tropical conditions.
*   **Conquest of the Cerrado**: Developing technologies that made the acidic soil of the Cerrado highly productive, opening new agricultural frontiers.
*   **Food Sovereignty**: Ensuring the country's food security and contributing to global food supply.

The work of Mariangela Hungria and many other Embrapa researchers exemplifies how science and innovation are vital for the sustainable development of agribusiness, positioning Brazil at the forefront of responsible food production.

## Conclusion

Mariangela Hungria's trajectory is an inspiration and a testament to the power of applied scientific research. Her legacy in BNF has not only boosted the Brazilian economy but also set new sustainability standards for global agriculture. Embrapa, in turn, continues to be the backbone of this innovation, proving that investing in science is investing in the future of agribusiness and humanity.
